With CMK and offline requirements, I could use envelope encryption—cache a derived key locally that's encrypted by the CMK—but that means I can't rotate keys during offline periods, and pre-cached keys shift control away from the customer. The 50ms latency with synchronous audit logging is also nearly impossible; async logging would work but breaks the guarantee that logs are durable before the response returns. These need to be escalated as conflicting constraints. The real tension emerges when synchronous field access logging conflicts with the 50ms latency requirement—if audit completeness demands blocking writes, the performance budget becomes impossible. So the genuine contradictions are between CMK and offline capabilities, and between the latency constraint and comprehensive audit logging. I should design the architecture to surface these trade-offs explicitly.
