Let me think through the failure modes systematically. Architecture: 1. Order Service → Payment Service (sync HTTP) 2. Payment Service → Stripe (sync HTTP) 3. Order Service → Notification Service (async fire-and-forget HTTP POST) Failure modes: **Chain 1-2: Order → Payment → Stripe (synchronous)** - Payment Service down → Order hangs/fails - Stripe down → Payment hangs → Order hangs (cascading) - Stripe latency cascades back through Payment to Order, slowing the entire flow - Network partitions at any hop break the chain - Ambiguous Stripe responses (timeouts after charges complete) create double-charge risks - Payment Service crashes mid-transaction leaves orders in an inconsistent state where charges went through but weren't confirmed - Slow Stripe calls exhaust Payment Service's thread and connection pools, blocking all subsequent orders **Chain 3: Order → Notification (fire-and-forget)** - Notification Service failures silently drop emails with no retry mechanism or delivery guarantee - Order Service doesn't wait for acknowledgment, so it has no visibility into whether notifications actually reached users - Partial failures in Notification Service can cause duplicate or missing emails - No dead letter queue means lost notifications are unrecoverable **Cross-cutting issues:** - Missing idempotency means retries trigger duplicate charges - No circuit breakers allow failures to cascade through the system - Unbounded latency from missing timeout budgets compounds the problem - The synchronous chain makes overall Order availability dependent on the product of Order, Payment, and Stripe availability
