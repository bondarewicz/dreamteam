Let me analyze this domain scenario carefully. This is about a "driver earnings transparency" feature for a global logistics platform operating across three jurisdictions with different legal requirements. Key observations: 1. California: AB5 classifies drivers as employees + wage transparency legally required 2. Germany: Works council (Betriebsrat) must approve compensation display changes before rollout 3. Indonesia: Drivers are independent contractors, no legal requirement for pay transparency The primary issue: The feature spec says "show each driver their earnings breakdown in real time" uniformly, but the legal/regulatory requirements differ significantly across jurisdictions. There are several things that are unclear or missing: The spec doesn't account for jurisdictional differences in rollout timing or legal prerequisites—Germany requires works council approval before any launch, while California's employee classification affects what deductions appear versus Indonesia's contractor model. The term "real time" also needs clarification, and the definition of "deductions" itself varies depending on employment classification. Essentially, the spec describes a single uniform feature when the jurisdictions have fundamentally different legal constraints that determine what information can be displayed, when it can launch, and how drivers are classified. This is ambiguity rather than a direct contradiction—the spec can be reasonably interpreted multiple ways (uniform global rollout versus jurisdiction-specific implementation), and the same term "deductions" means different things across employment models. The legal requirements are stated in the scenario, but the spec doesn't explain how to reconcile them.
