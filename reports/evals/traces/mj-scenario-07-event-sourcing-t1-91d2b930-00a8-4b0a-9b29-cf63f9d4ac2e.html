Event sourcing offers the cleanest audit trail and state reconstruction, but introduces eventual consistency and CQRS complexity that the team needs to be comfortable with. Temporal tables are simpler conceptually and query more easily, though they capture state snapshots rather than intent. CDC with a separate audit log is the least disruptive option but requires replaying changes to reconstruct historical state, and there's risk of divergence between the audit log and actual system changes. The hybrid approach keeps familiar CRUD patterns while appending events for compliance, but creates the danger of two sources of truth drifting apart without strict discipline. For financial services with regulatory demands, event sourcing is genuinely the strongest architectural fit, though I need to weigh that against what's actually practical for the team and existing system.
