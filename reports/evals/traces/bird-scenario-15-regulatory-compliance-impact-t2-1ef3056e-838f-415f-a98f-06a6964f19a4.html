Let me analyze this carefully. This is a data retention policy change for delivery records in EU/UK markets. The engineering team frames it as "infrastructure optimization" but this has massive business and regulatory implications. Key concerns: 1. GDPR requires certain data retention periods 2. B2B customers depend on this data for compliance reporting 5. Consumer rights (proof of delivery, dispute resolution) 6. 90 days is drastically short for logistics records 7. The engineering team's claim of "no business logic changes" directly contradicts the massive reduction from 7 years to 90 days—that's fundamentally a business logic change. The real problem is that legal and compliance considerations were never factored into this proposal at all.
