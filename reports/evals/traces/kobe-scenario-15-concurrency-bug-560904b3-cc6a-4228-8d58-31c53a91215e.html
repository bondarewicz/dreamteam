However, since JS is single-threaded with an event loop, the increments themselves won't interleave mid-operation. BUT - the `++` operator is read-modify-write, and if `await processDelivery(id)` yields, multiple promises resume and increment. Actually in JS, even though it's single-threaded, `results.processed++` is: read value, increment, write back. Between any two awaits, only one microtask runs.
