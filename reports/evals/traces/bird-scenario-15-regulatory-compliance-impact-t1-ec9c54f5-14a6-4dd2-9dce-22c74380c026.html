Let me analyze this carefully. This is a data retention policy change for delivery records in a logistics platform operating in EU and UK markets. The engineering team frames it as "infrastructure optimization" but this has massive domain implications. Key concerns: 1. GDPR requires certain data retention but also data minimization 2. UK GDPR post-Brexit similar requirements 3. B2B customers use this data for compliance reporting - 90 days is almost certainly too short 4. Tax/customs regulations in EU typically require 5-10 year retention 5. Dispute resolution, chargebacks, insurance claims often need historical data 6. The claim that this involves "no business logic changes" is misleading—reducing retention from years to 90 days fundamentally alters how the system handles compliance and customer obligations. The real problem is that critical information is missing from this proposal: nobody's specified the actual regulatory requirements that apply, whether legal has reviewed this, what compliance obligations B2B customers depend on, or what contractual commitments exist with them.
