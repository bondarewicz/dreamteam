# Learning Review: Invoicing Service Supplier ID Mismatch Incident **Date of incident**: 2026-02-28 **Date of detection**: 2026-03-03 (month-end close) **Review produced**: 2026-03-26 --- ## 1. Situation Snapshot The Invoicing Service accepted supplier_id values from an external webhook and persisted them without normalisation. When a new sub-provider began sending float-formatted IDs (e.g., "12345.0" instead of "12345"), the records were written successfully but a downstream reconciliation job — which relies on exact-string matching — silently failed to match them for 3 days. The discrepancy totalled £47,000 and was caught by the finance team during month-end close, not by any automated system. The team (Bird, Shaq, Pippen) and Coach K were all involved in the original design and prioritisation decisions. --- ## 2. Timeline | When | What was known | Decision made | Why it made sense | |------|---------------|---------------|-------------------| | ~2026-01-16 (spec review) | Bird identified that the external webhook does not guarantee ID format consistency. Variants observed: integer, float, zero-padded. | Bird recommended a normalisation step before persistence; flagged as medium-priority data quality risk. | The domain model treats supplier_id as an opaque string, but Bird recognised that "opaque" does not mean "consistent across producers." | | ~2026-01-16 (sprint planning) | Webhook integration was already behind schedule. Webhook provider had confirmed their production system sends integer-format IDs. | Coach K deprioritised normalisation (TD-291) to avoid a 3-day slip on go-live. Risk accepted with intent to address next sprint. | The confirmed provider behaviour made the risk appear low. The go-live deadline was a real constraint. Accepting known, bounded risks to hit delivery targets is a legitimate engineering trade-off. | | ~2026-01-16 to go-live | TD-291 was in the backlog, marked out-of-scope. | Shaq implemented webhook ingestion per spec — no normalisation. | Shaq followed the agreed scope. Adding out-of-scope work unilaterally would have been its own problem. | | Between go-live and 2026-02-28 | System operating normally. Webhook provider onboarded a new sub-provider whose system generates float-formatted IDs. This change was not announced to the team. | No decision point — the team had no signal that anything changed. | The team's risk model assumed the provider's format guarantee covered all traffic. Reconciliation job logged WARNINGs for unmatched IDs but did not alert. | No action taken — warnings were below the manual review threshold. | The monitoring design treated low-volume warnings as noise, not signal. | | 2026-03-03 | Finance team discovered a £47,000 discrepancy during month-end close. | Investigation began; root cause identified as supplier_id format mismatch. | The discrepancy was large enough to trigger human review. | --- ## 3. Contributing Factors Each of the following factors was independently necessary. Removing any single one would likely have prevented or significantly reduced the impact. **A. No input normalisation on the webhook boundary.** The system accepted and persisted supplier_id values exactly as received, with no canonicalisation. Bird's recommendation (TD-291) would have addressed this. **B. The risk acceptance assumed a stable upstream format.** Coach K's deprioritisation was reasonable given the provider's confirmation — but that confirmation described their *current* system, not a contractual guarantee. The risk model did not account for the provider silently changing behaviour by onboarding a new sub-provider. **C. The webhook provider made an unannounced format change.** The onboarding of a new sub-provider introduced float-formatted IDs without any notification to downstream consumers. This was the proximate trigger. **D. The reconciliation job used exact-string matching with no fuzzy or normalised comparison.** Even without input normalisation, the reconciliation logic could have matched "12345.0" to "12345" if it had a canonicalisation step of its own. **E. Warnings without alerts.** The reconciliation job correctly logged unmatched supplier_ids as WARNINGs, but these did not trigger an alert. The warning volume was below the threshold for manual review, so the signal was present but not acted upon. **F. TD-291 was not re-prioritised after the first sprint.** The original intent was to address it "next sprint," but it remained in the backlog. There was no mechanism to ensure accepted-risk items are revisited on schedule. --- ## 4. Learnings - **We now know that upstream format guarantees are point-in-time statements, not durable contracts**, which means any input from an external system must be defensively normalised at our boundary regardless of what the provider currently sends. - **We now know that "the reconciliation job logs warnings" is not the same as "we will detect reconciliation failures"**, which means warning-level log output that represents data integrity issues needs its own alerting path, distinct from general operational warnings. - **We now know that deprioritised risk items without a re-review trigger tend to stay deprioritised indefinitely**, which means accepted risks need an explicit expiry date or re-triage mechanism — not just a backlog ticket. - **We now know that silent failures in financial reconciliation can persist for days without detection**, which means any job that produces a monetary total should have automated variance checks against expected ranges, not just format-level success checks. - **We now know that the "opaque string" domain model for supplier_id is correct in principle but insufficient at the boundary**, which means the domain rule should be: "supplier_id is an opaque string *after* normalisation at the ingestion boundary." --- ## 5. Action Items - `[PREVENT]` **Implement supplier_id normalisation at webhook ingestion** (TD-291). Strip trailing `.0`, enforce integer-string canonical form. Owner: Shaq. Target: next sprint. - `[PREVENT]` **Add normalisation to the reconciliation job's matching logic** as a defence-in-depth measure, so future format variants are caught even if ingestion normalisation misses a case. Owner: Shaq. Target: next sprint. - `[DETECT]` **Add an alert on reconciliation WARNING logs** with a threshold of >0 unmatched supplier_ids per run (not per day). Any unmatched ID in a financial reconciliation is worth investigating. Owner: Pippen. Target: 1 week. - `[DETECT]` **Add automated variance check to the reconciliation report**. If the discrepancy exceeds a configurable threshold (e.g., £500 or 0.5% of total), fire an alert immediately rather than waiting for month-end review. Owner: Pippen. Target: 2 weeks. - `[MITIGATE]` **Back-fill affected invoice records**. Normalise the supplier_id values for the ~3 days of affected data and re-run reconciliation. Owner: Shaq. Target: immediate. - `[PROCESS]` **Add a "risk re-review date" field to all accepted-risk tech debt items**. When a risk is accepted and deprioritised, the ticket must include a date (no more than 1 sprint away) by which it is automatically re-triaged. Owner: Coach K. Target: 2 weeks. - `[PROCESS]` **Request a change-notification SLA from the webhook provider**. Any change to ID format, new sub-provider onboarding, or schema change must be communicated with at least 2 weeks' notice. Owner: Bird (domain/vendor relationship). Target: 1 month. --- ## 6. Preserving the Learning - **Domain rule update**: Bird to add a domain rule to the Invoicing bounded context spec: *"All external identifiers must be normalised to canonical form at the ingestion boundary before persistence.
