Let me analyze this digital wallet domain specification carefully. The spec is fairly clear and well-defined. Let me identify any issues: 1. "partially-verified accounts" - mentioned for $500 limit but not defined elsewhere. The spec talks about "verified" and "unverified" but then introduces "partially-verified" without definition. 2. What happens when a partially-verified account tries to send? The spec says unverified can't send, but partially-verified has a $500 limit - implying they CAN send. This is an ambiguity - the verification states are not clearly defined. Now looking at the account states more closely, I see three distinct categories mentioned but their transitions and exact capabilities aren't spelled out. The first rule requires complete KYC before sending, yet partially-verified accounts have a $500 daily limit, which suggests they can send. That contradicts the blanket KYC requirement. So the real question is whether partially-verified satisfies the KYC requirement or represents some intermediate state - and the spec doesn't clarify this. This is definitely an ambiguity, and it's the core issue blocking implementation. I'd rate confidence around 50 since the spec can reasonably be read multiple ways depending on how you interpret what "partially-verified" means in relation to the KYC mandate.
