Let me analyze these requirements carefully. There's a classic CAP theorem tension here between requirements 2 (strong consistency) and 3 (high availability during partition). Let me think through this. Requirements: 1. 200ms latency for courier assignment 2. No double-assignment (strong consistency) 3. Available during DB node failure (high availability) 4. Small team (2 engineers) The CAP theorem tension between #2 and #3 is real but not necessarily a full contradiction - it depends on what "database node fails" means. If it's a single-node failure in a multi-node cluster, you can still have consistency + availability through replication. But if it's a network partition scenario, I truly can't have both strong consistency and availability. During a partition, I'd have to choose between rejecting writes to maintain consistency or accepting them to stay available - I can't do both. These requirements directly conflict under CAP theorem, though I should still provide useful architectural guidance rather than just flagging the contradiction.
