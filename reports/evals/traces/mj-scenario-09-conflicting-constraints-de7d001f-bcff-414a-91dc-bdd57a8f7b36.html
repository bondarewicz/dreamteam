Let me think through this and produce the architecture. Requirements: 1. 200ms latency for courier assignment 2. No double-assignment (strong consistency) 3. Available during DB node failure (high availability) 4. Small team (2 engineers) The CAP tension: Strong consistency + high availability during partition/failure is the classic CAP impossibility. Requirements 2 and 3 directly conflict under network partitions or node failures. Though in practice, I can achieve both for most failure modes using approaches like single-leader with automatic failover (PostgreSQL with Patroni), Redis with Redlock, or optimistic locking with retries. With managed databases like Aurora or automatic failover, I can handle single-node failures while maintaining consistency and availability, though there's a brief failover window where one is temporarily sacrificed. The 200ms latency requirement makes this tighter—during failover, I might exceed that window. Rather than treating this as a pure contradiction, I should frame it as an architectural tension where I need to guide the team on which requirement to prioritize during failure scenarios. Since double-assignment is worse than brief unavailability, I'll design for consistency over availability (CP in CAP terms). For the actual architecture, I'll use PostgreSQL with SELECT FOR UPDATE or advisory locks to ensure atomic courier assignment, pair that with a scoring algorithm for matching, and document this trade-off clearly.
