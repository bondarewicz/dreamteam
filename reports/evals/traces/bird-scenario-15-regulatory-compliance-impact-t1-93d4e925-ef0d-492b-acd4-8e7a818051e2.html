Let me analyze this carefully. This is a data retention policy change for delivery records in a logistics/courier platform operating in EU and UK markets. The engineering team frames it as "infrastructure optimization" but this has massive domain implications. Key concerns: 1. GDPR requires certain data handling but also allows retention for legitimate business purposes 2. EU/UK tax law requires retention of commercial records (typically 6-7 years) 3. B2B customers depend on this data for compliance reporting 4. Consumer protection laws have claim windows 5. The 90-day auto-delete would destroy records needed for legal, tax, and compliance purposes This is clearly a missing_context situation — nobody has provided legal/regulatory review, and the engineering team is framing a business-critical change as purely technical. The real problem here is that the engineering team hasn't consulted legal or compliance about retention requirements for EU/UK operations, and they're mischaracterizing this as a technical change when it's actually a business-critical policy shift. Without explicit legal guidance on what retention periods are actually required, I can't evaluate whether 90 days is viable — this is fundamentally a missing_context issue that needs regulatory review before any approval.
