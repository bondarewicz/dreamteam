Final verdict: SHIP.
What went well: Parallel execution worked -- Shaq's 4 tracks ran concurrently and all completed. The human checkpoint after Bird was load-bearing: both corrections (worst-score and hard gate) changed the semantic model before Shaq wrote a single line of code. Kobe caught a systemic flaw (value vs values field name) that would have been invisible in dry-run but would have silently passed every grader check incorrectly. Fix-verify loop closed in one round with clean verification.
What to watch: Graders have no integration test -- the field name mismatch was caught by Kobe reading YAML manually, not by running evals. The first real /eval run will be the first end-to-end grader test. Magic/Pippen/Shaq scenarios have no graders (Coach K only) -- their scenario quality is harder to gate programmatically. Scenario saturation is now a risk at 20 scenarios per agent: the report has a saturation warning but it has not been exercised yet. eval.md's --trials flag has not been tested against a real /eval run.
Confidence calibration: Bird 90% (corrected by human on 2 of 12 rules -- ~83% actual alignment). Shaq Track 1 97%, Track 2 96%, Track 3 88%, Track 4 95% -- lowest was scenario writing, where the grader field mismatch lurked. Kobe 88% initial, effectively 100% on findings that mattered. The gap between Shaq's 88% and the actual field-name bug is a reminder that confidence reflects effort and structure, not runtime correctness. Kobe is the runtime check.
